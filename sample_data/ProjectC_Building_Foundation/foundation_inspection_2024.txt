INSPECTION TYPE: Forensic Foundation Investigation - Settlement Complaint

=== BACKGROUND ===
The owner (Riverside Commerce LLC) reported observable settlement and cracking in the building structure. The building is a 3-story commercial building built in 2018. Building area: 42,000 sq ft. Foundation type: Shallow spread footings on native soil.

Complaints received:
- Visible diagonal cracking in exterior masonry walls
- Doors on the east wing not closing properly (frame distortion)
- Sloping floors on Level 1 east wing (measured up to 1.5 inches over 20 ft)
- Interior partition wall cracks (multiple locations)

=== INVESTIGATION SCOPE ===
1. Visual inspection of structure
2. Floor elevation survey
3. Review of original geotechnical report
4. Soil borings (4 new borings — B-1 through B-4)
5. Laboratory testing of soil samples

=== VISUAL INSPECTION FINDINGS ===

EXTERIOR:
- Stair-step cracking in brick veneer at northeast corner — significant (crack widths to 1/4 inch)
- Settlement crack at building re-entrant corner (east elevation) — vertical offset 3/8 inch
- Concrete apron slab at loading dock: settled 2.5 inches, cracked
- Window frames distorted — east wing, floors 1 and 2

INTERIOR (Level 1):
- Grid Lines D-F (east portion): Diagonal cracks in drywall — consistent with differential settlement
- Column bases at Grid D-4 and D-5: Visual gap forming between column baseplate and grout
- Hardwood flooring in lease space A: Buckled — 1.2 inch rise over 8 feet

INTERIOR (Level 2 and 3):
- Less severe cracking — pattern consistent with settlement in foundation, not superstructure

=== FLOOR ELEVATION SURVEY ===
Survey performed by licensed surveyor (attached — DEST-2024-078-SURVEY)
Key findings:
- Maximum settlement measured: 3.2 inches (northeast corner, Grid D-F area)
- Settlement gradient: 1.5 inches over 15 feet at worst location
- Settlement pattern: Consistent with localized soil compression in northeast quadrant

=== GEOTECHNICAL FINDINGS ===

Original 2017 Geotechnical Report (Soil Tech Inc.):
- Native soil: Sandy silt with clay layers
- Bearing capacity assumed: 1,500 psf
- No groundwater encountered (borings to 30 ft)
- No expansive soil testing performed (CONCERN)

New Borings (February 2024):
Boring B-1 (northeast corner — settlement area):
- 0-3 ft: Fill material (construction debris mixed with clay) — NOT in original report
- 3-12 ft: Sandy silt, loose to medium dense
- 12-25 ft: Clay layer, soft to medium — plasticity index = 42 (HIGH)
- 25-35 ft: Dense sand (suitable bearing)

Boring B-2 (center of building):
- 0-2 ft: Fill
- 2-15 ft: Sandy silt, medium dense
- 15-35 ft: Dense sand
Note: No significant clay layer — explains why center is not settling

Laboratory Results:
- B-1 clay sample: Liquid Limit = 58, Plastic Limit = 16, PI = 42
- Swell test: 4.2% swell under 200 psf surcharge — EXPANSIVE SOIL
- Consolidation test: Compression index Cc = 0.31 (moderate consolidation potential)

=== ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS ===
PRIMARY CAUSE: Undisclosed fill material (0-3 ft) combined with soft clay layer (12-25 ft) not identified in original geotechnical investigation. The soft clay layer is undergoing consolidation settlement under building loads.

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS:
1. Expansive clay reacting to moisture fluctuations (irrigation system nearby)
2. Original geotechnical report did not include exploratory borings in the northeast quadrant
3. Foundation design may have been inadequate for actual soil conditions

=== SETTLEMENT PROGNOSIS ===
Based on consolidation analysis:
- Estimated remaining settlement: 1.5 - 2.5 inches additional
- Estimated time to reach 90% consolidation: 18-36 months
- Settlement will continue without remediation

=== RECOMMENDED REMEDIATION ===

Option 1 (Recommended): Underpinning with Helical Piers
- Install helical piers to dense sand layer (approximately 30 ft depth)
- Estimated 28 piers at northeast quadrant
- Lift and re-level building after pier installation
- Estimated cost: $380,000 - $450,000

Option 2: Grout Injection (Compaction Grouting)
- Fill and densify loose zone (0-12 ft)
- Less effective for deep clay consolidation
- Estimated cost: $180,000 - $240,000 (may not be sufficient)

RECOMMENDATION: Proceed with Option 1 (Helical Piers) for long-term stability.

=== IMMEDIATE SAFETY CONCERNS ===
- Monitor column baseplates at D-4 and D-5 — install settlement monitoring points
- Do not occupy east wing Level 1 tenant space A until structural engineer reviews
- Restrict heavy equipment/loading in northeast quadrant
