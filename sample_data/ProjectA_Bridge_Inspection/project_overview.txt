PROJECT OVERVIEW DOCUMENT
=========================
Project Name: SR-45 Bridge Replacement / Rehabilitation Study
Project ID: DEST-2024-001
Client: Riverside County Department of Transportation
Engineer of Record: Distinct Engineering Solutions, Inc.
Project Manager: Michael Torres, P.E.

STRUCTURE DETAILS:
- Bridge Name: Millbrook Creek Crossing
- Route: State Route 45
- Structure Type: 3-Span Continuous Steel Plate Girder
- Year Built: 1987
- Total Length: 210 feet (3 spans: 65ft + 80ft + 65ft)
- Width: 44 feet (2 lanes + shoulders)
- Design Load: HS-20
- Foundation Type: Drilled Shaft on Pier 1, Spread Footing on Pier 2
- Waterway: Millbrook Creek (drainage area: 145 sq mi)
- FEMA Flood Zone: AE (100-year floodplain)

PROJECT HISTORY:
- 1987: Original construction
- 1998: Deck overlay (Type B modified)
- 2009: Bearing pad replacement (all locations)
- 2015: Scour countermeasures installed (Pier 1)
- 2019: Paint system applied to steel girders
- 2023: Pier 1 cap crack repair

CURRENT PROJECT SCOPE:
The county has initiated a Rehabilitation Study to determine whether the bridge should be:
Option A: Major Rehabilitation (deck replacement, girder painting, substructure repairs)
Option B: Full Replacement (new bridge with wider deck for future traffic growth)

Preliminary cost estimates:
- Option A: $2.1M - $2.8M
- Option B: $8.5M - $11.2M

TRAFFIC DATA:
- Current ADT: 4,200 vehicles/day
- Truck percentage: 8%
- 20-year projected ADT: 6,800 vehicles/day

ENVIRONMENTAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Millbrook Creek is designated as a sensitive habitat (steelhead trout)
- Any in-water work requires CDFW 1602 permit
- Work window: July 1 - September 30 (fish passage window)
